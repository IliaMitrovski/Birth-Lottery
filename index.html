An interactive installation about unequal starting conditions

BIRTH PRIVILEGE

Same effort. Different starting point. Different outcome.

Birth privilege describes advantages and disadvantages that exist before personal choices begin. Place of birth, family background, and access to resources can shape opportunity.

The installation contrasts physical and digital work to show how comparable effort can receive very different value.

Why starting conditions matter

Effort does not begin on equal ground.

  • Birthplace shapes access to infrastructure, institutions, and markets.
  • Family shapes financial security, education, and social support.
  • Access shapes which opportunities exist before effort begins.

Why two kinds of work?

The installation makes unequal conditions visible.

Physical side

Visible effort. Physical strain.

Movement and repetition represent work that may be demanding, insecure, or weakly protected.

Digital side

Access, comfort, and leverage.

The digital task represents work supported by technology, education, infrastructure, and better-paid markets.

This is an artistic simplification. Global North and Global South are broad socio-economic terms, not fixed geographical borders.

A question to take with you

How much of success is determined by effort — and how much is determined before we are even born?

Birth Privilege asks us to question what society calls merit, success, and fairness.
